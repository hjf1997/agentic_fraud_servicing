# Merchant Dispute Case Opening Checklist

This document defines the criteria, merchant contact requirements, and blocking rules
for opening a **merchant dispute case** (authorized transaction with a merchant complaint)
on an AMEX card account.

## Required Information

The following information **must** be collected before a merchant dispute can be opened:

- **Merchant name**: the name of the merchant as it appears on the statement
- **Transaction amount**: the full or partial amount being disputed
- **Date of purchase**: the date the original transaction occurred
- **Nature of complaint**: one of the following categories:
  - Goods not received
  - Goods not as described or defective
  - Duplicate or incorrect charge
  - Service not rendered
  - Recurring charge after cancellation
  - Credit or refund not processed

## Merchant Contact Requirement

The cardholder **must** have attempted to resolve the issue directly with the merchant
before AMEX will open a dispute case:

- **Contact attempt required**: the cardholder must confirm they have contacted the merchant (phone, email, in-person, or through the merchant's website) and were unable to reach a resolution
- **Documentation preferred**: if the cardholder has written correspondence with the merchant (emails, chat logs, return receipts), note this in the case file
- **Exception — merchant no longer in business**: if the merchant has permanently closed, ceased operations, or is unreachable after reasonable attempts, the merchant contact requirement is **waived**. The CCP should document the evidence of closure (e.g., website down, phone disconnected, news reports).
- **Exception — merchant unresponsive for 15+ days**: if the cardholder can demonstrate they contacted the merchant more than 15 days ago with no response, the requirement is considered met

## Eligibility Rules

A merchant dispute may only be opened if **all** of the following conditions are met:

- **Statement date window**: the dispute must be filed within **60 days** of the statement date on which the transaction appeared
- **Minimum dispute amount**: the disputed amount must be at least **$25.00**. Amounts below $25.00 are not eligible for the dispute process (advise cardholder to contact merchant directly).
- **Transaction type**: the transaction must be a completed purchase (not a pending authorization). Pending authorizations should be monitored and disputed after posting.

## Blocking Rules

A merchant dispute **cannot** be opened if any of the following conditions apply:

- **Recurring charges without cancellation proof**: for disputes involving recurring or subscription charges, the cardholder must provide evidence of cancellation (confirmation email, cancellation number, screenshot). Without cancellation proof, the dispute cannot proceed.
- **Refund already issued**: if the merchant has already issued a refund or credit for the disputed amount, a dispute case cannot be opened. Advise the cardholder to wait **5-10 business days** for the refund to post.
- **Active fraud case**: if the same transaction has an active fraud case, it cannot simultaneously be opened as a merchant dispute. The fraud case must be closed or withdrawn first.
- **Cash-equivalent transactions**: cash advances, money orders, wire transfers, and cryptocurrency purchases are **not eligible** for merchant disputes.
